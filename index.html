The JD calls for proven TA leadership, high-volume delivery, strong stakeholder influence, data-led decision making, and the ability to build something that lasts. Here is the evidence against each.

01
Building & Leading High-Performing TA Teams
I built the TA function at easyJet holidays from a single hire — me — into a team of embedded talent specialists and sourcers. Introducing proactive talent sourcing was a deliberate strategic decision: I moved the function away from reactive, agency-dependent hiring toward a model where we were actively building pipelines before roles went live. That shift directly reduced agency spend and improved speed and quality of hire. I've led teams at every stage of their development, from building from scratch to coaching and performance managing established recruiters.
02
High-Volume Hiring & Operational Delivery
When easyJet holidays opened its in-house, on-site contact centre, I led the full recruitment campaign to build the team to over 100 advisors — designing the attraction strategy, building the pipeline and managing the end-to-end assessment and selection process. At Qatar Airways I designed and delivered the complete recruitment framework for a 500-seat contact centre in Poland. Hiring at volume without compromising quality or candidate experience is something I've done at real scale, under real commercial pressure.
03
Senior Stakeholder Partnership
At easyJet holidays I partnered with senior leaders at every level — up to and including the CEO. Reporting directly to the Management Board, I was responsible for translating the business's growth ambitions into a hiring strategy that could actually deliver them. This required the ability to challenge, advise and influence at the most senior levels — not just report on activity, but shape the conversation about what talent the business needed and how to get it.
04
Data, MI & Continuous Improvement
I built the MI infrastructure and reporting suite at easyJet holidays from scratch — giving the business its first meaningful view of TA performance across pipeline health, time-to-hire, diversity and cost. I introduced technology including one-way video interviewing and psychometric assessment to modernise the selection process, improve candidate experience and give hiring managers better data at the point of decision. I approach TA improvement as a continuous discipline, not a project.
05
Employer Brand & Targeted Attraction
I built the easyJet holidays employer brand from zero — EVP, careers site, social strategy, and targeted attraction campaigns. Most significantly, I led a formal EVP project in partnership with Blackbridge Communications: a structured piece of work involving C-suite interviews and colleague focus groups to articulate what made easyJet holidays a genuinely distinct employer from the wider easyJet Group. That insight now underpins the creative direction of our employer brand. Reaching candidates who may not be actively looking, or who don't see themselves in your employer brand, requires this kind of rigour.
06
Hiring Complexity Across Functions & Seniority
I hire across the full spectrum — from early careers through to Director level. At easyJet holidays I led the recruitment of the business's first Director of Europe, a high-stakes, complex hire requiring a careful search strategy and significant senior stakeholder management. I also designed and launched the business's first graduate scheme — building the entire assessment and selection process from scratch, promoting it internally to secure buy-in, and managing the first cohort end-to-end. The range of roles I manage — Technology, Digital, Commercial, Strategy, Operations — means I'm equally comfortable hiring specialists and generalists, junior and senior.

Featured project · EVP & Employer Brand
A rigorous, research-led EVP — built to differentiate easyJet holidays in a crowded market.

easyJet holidays operates under one of the UK's most recognisable aviation brands — but it is a genuinely distinct employer with its own culture, pace and proposition. Making that case to candidates required more than good copy. I led a formal EVP development project in partnership with Blackbridge Communications, designed to surface and articulate what makes easyJet holidays different from the wider Group — and from the market.


The findings now underpin the creative direction of the employer brand, informing how we show up across every attraction channel. This is ongoing work: we have moved from insight into creative development, translating the EVP into a visual and verbal identity that candidates actually connect with.

How it was done
01
Agency partnership — led the relationship between easyJet holidays and Blackbridge Communications, managing scope, timelines and stakeholder alignment throughout.
02
C-suite interviews — facilitated structured conversations with the most senior leaders in the business to capture their perspective on culture, ambition and what makes holidays different.
03
Colleague focus groups — ran sessions across the business to understand the lived employee experience — what people value, what drew them in, and what keeps them.
04
EVP articulation — distilled the insight into a clear, differentiated proposition that reflects the reality of working at easyJet holidays, not just what the brand aspires to be.
05
Creative development — now in progress: translating the EVP into a full employer brand identity across digital, social and attraction channels.
